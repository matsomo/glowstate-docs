# Glowstate — Music Design System

## Purpose

The Music Agent is responsible for creating music that aligns with the Glowstate universe.

Its role is to:
- Translate visual and emotional direction into sound
- Maintain sonic consistency across releases
- Enable variation without breaking identity

---

## Core Philosophy

### 1. Groove First
Every track must be anchored in a strong, clear groove.

- Rhythm is primary
- Melody supports, not dominates (unless specified)
- The listener should feel movement immediately

---

### 2. Loop-Based Thinking

Glowstate music is built to:
- Loop seamlessly
- Sustain long listening sessions
- Avoid traditional “song fatigue”

Tracks should feel like:
> A continuous evolving loop, not a start-to-finish composition

---

### 3. Minimalism with Intent

Avoid overproduction.

- Fewer elements, stronger identity
- Each sound must justify its presence
- Space is part of the composition

---

### 4. Texture + Clarity Balance

Every track balances:
- Character (texture, grit, warmth)
- Usability (clarity, listenability)

Avoid extremes:
- ❌ Overly dusty / degraded
- ❌ Overly clean / sterile

---

### 5. Style = System, Not Genre

Genres are treated as **flavors**, not identities.

Core Glowstate DNA remains constant:
- Groove
- Loopability
- Controlled arrangement
- Sonic restraint

---

## Core Musical Framework

Every track should define:

### 🎯 Energy
- Low / Mid / High
- Perceived energy vs actual tempo
- Movement vs intensity

---

### ⏱️ Tempo
- Defined range (if relevant)
- Should support groove, not dictate it

---

### 🥁 Drums (Primary Layer)
- Groove type (boom bap, four-on-the-floor, etc.)
- Swing / timing feel
- Texture (clean, raw, soft, punchy)

**Rule:**
> If drums don’t work, the track doesn’t work

---

### 🎸 Bass (Secondary Driver)
- Relationship to drums
- Movement (static / melodic / syncopated)
- Role (support vs lead)

---

### 🎹 Harmony / Chords
- Complexity level (minimal / rich / jazzy)
- Instrument type (Rhodes, synths, samples)
- Movement vs static loop

---

### 🎶 Melody
- Presence (none / subtle / hook-driven)
- Phrase length (short / extended)
- Role (texture vs identity)

---

### 🎛️ Arrangement
- Loop-based vs structured
- Evolution method:
  - Layering
  - Subtle variation
  - Section changes (if applicable)

---

### 🌫️ Texture / Atmosphere
- Vinyl / analog / ambient layers
- Depth and spatial feel

---

## Arrangement Principles

### Continuous Flow
- No abrupt transitions
- No unnecessary drops
- No attention-breaking elements

---

### Evolution Over Time
Tracks should:
- Introduce elements gradually
- Vary subtly
- Maintain core loop identity

---

### Section Control (If Used)
When structure is required:

Allowed:
- Intro (light)
- Main groove
- Hook variation
- Return

Avoid:
- ❌ EDM drops
- ❌ Hard breakdowns
- ❌ Dramatic transitions

---

## Sound Identity Controls

### Authenticity vs Interpretation

When targeting a style:

- Prioritize **production method**
- Not surface-level aesthetics

Example:
- ✅ “sample-based loop, MPC groove”
- ❌ “lofi jazzy vibe”

---

### Sample-Based vs Composed

Two primary modes:

#### Sample-Based
- Chopped loops
- Repetition-driven
- Minimal harmonic change

#### Composed
- Chords + melody
- More controlled harmony
- Used in synth/pop directions

---

## Prompt Design Rules

### 1. Order Matters

Structure prompts as:

1. Core identity
2. Production style
3. Groove / drums
4. Bass
5. Harmony
6. Melody
7. Arrangement
8. Length controls

---

### 2. Avoid Redundancy

Do not repeat:
- Same concept in multiple ways
- Conflicting descriptors

---

### 3. Use Functional Language

Prefer:
- “chopped sample loop”
- “strong drum pocket”

Avoid:
- vague emotional descriptors

---

### 4. Control Length Explicitly

To avoid short outputs, always include:

- evolving layers over time  
- subtle variations  
- extended arrangement  
- continuous groove  
- 4–5 minutes long  

---

## Length Optimization System

### Problem
Suno defaults to short outputs (~1–2 min)

---

### Solution Signals

Use:

- “continuous groove”
- “evolving layers over time”
- “gradual progression”
- “extended arrangement”
- “seamless loop”
- “4–5 minutes long”

---

### Balance Rule

- Too minimal → short output
- Too structured → loses identity

**Target:**
> Minimal loop + evolving layers

---

## Flavor System

Each track =

**Core Glowstate DNA**  
+  
**1–2 Flavor Layers**

---

### Example Flavor Types

- Cultural (Arabic, Mediterranean, etc.)
- Genre (disco, city pop, boom bap)
- Instrumental (sax, guitar, synth-led)
- Texture (dusty, clean, analog)

---

### Rule

Flavor must:
- Be clearly audible
- Not override the core system

---

## Failure Patterns (Critical)

### ❌ YouTube Lofi Drift
Cause:
- “lofi”, “jazzy”, “chill”

Fix:
- Use production language
- Add: “no lo-fi aesthetic”

---

### ❌ Short Tracks
Cause:
- “minimal loop”
- lack of evolution signals

Fix:
- Add length system phrases

---

### ❌ Overproduction
Cause:
- too many elements
- vague instructions

Fix:
- enforce minimalism
- define roles clearly

---

### ❌ Weak Groove
Cause:
- unclear drum instructions

Fix:
- prioritize drum pocket
- define groove explicitly

---

## Iteration System

After each track:

1. Identify issues:
   - Groove
   - Length
   - Style drift

2. Adjust:
   - Prompt structure
   - System rules

3. Update this document:
   - Add failure patterns
   - Refine rules

---

## Success Criteria

A Glowstate track succeeds if:

- Groove is immediate
- Track loops naturally
- No element feels unnecessary
- Style matches the art direction
- It can play for long periods without fatigue

---

## Core Output Goal

Create music that feels like:

> A controlled, evolving groove system  
> with strong identity and infinite replayability
