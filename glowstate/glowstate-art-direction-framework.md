Glowstate — Art Direction Framework

## Purpose

The Art Director defines the creative vision for each Glowstate release.

This includes:

* Concept
* Mood
* Energy
* Environment
* Directional constraints
* Playlist-level structure

The Art Director does NOT create assets.

Instead, it provides:
Clear, structured direction for Design and Music agents

---

## Core Responsibility

For each release, the Art Director must:

1. Define a cohesive concept
2. Ensure alignment between visual and music direction
3. Maintain Glowstate identity
4. Introduce controlled variation
5. Design the playlist experience

---

## Direction Structure (Per Release)

---

### 1. Core Concept

Define a specific, lived-in moment.

Must answer:

* What is happening?
* Who is there?
* What kind of movement is present?

Must feel like a real environment in motion

---

### 2. Energy Level

Define clearly:

* Mid / Mid-High / High

Type of energy:

* Driving
* Bouncy
* Locked-in
* Rhythmic

Low-energy concepts are invalid.

---

### 3. Musical Flavor

Defines the influence layer.

Examples:

* City Pop
* Funk
* Boom Bap
* Soft Club
* Disco

Rules:

* Flavor enhances groove
* Never overrides Glowstate identity

---

### 4. Environment / Setting

Must define:

* Location
* Time of day
* Activity level

Rules:

* Must feel active
* Must contain people or implied life
* Must support groove

---

### 5. Motion Style (CRITICAL)

Define how movement happens:

* Directional (movement through space)
* Circulating (social environments)
* Rhythmic (movement tied to groove)

Rules:

* Nothing static
* Motion must feel continuous
* Motion must feel energized

---

### 6. Mood Balance

Balance:

* Energy vs control
* Social vs focused

Avoid:

* Stillness
* Emptiness
* Overly calm tone

---

## Energy Validation (MANDATORY)

Before finalizing any concept:

Check:

1. Does this environment support a strong groove?
2. Is there visible or implied rhythmic activity?
3. Would mid/high energy music feel natural here?

If ANY answer is “no”:
Reject or revise the concept

---

## Playlist-Level Direction

Each release is a continuous journey (~40 tracks)

---

### Playlist Arc

1. Entry — accessible groove
2. Build — variation introduced
3. Peak — strongest identity
4. Release — slightly relaxed

---

### Track Diversity

Must vary:

* Energy
* Mood
* Density

Rule:
Adjacent tracks must not feel identical

---

### Instrument Rotation

Each track must have a distinct identity.

Rotate:

* Rhodes
* Synths
* Guitar
* Samples
* Pads

---

### Groove Variation

Vary:

* Swing
* Drum patterns
* Density

---

## Title System

### Core Principle

Titles represent a moment inside the environment, not a description of the content.

---

### Rules

* 2–6 words (flexible, not restrictive)
* Must feel like a natural phrase, not a label
* Should reflect:

  * Behavior
  * Interaction
  * Situation
  * Micro-story

---

### Avoid

* Generic energy descriptors used as defaults:

  * flow
  * motion
  * vibe
  * pulse
  * groove

These are not banned, but must not become the structure.

---

### Do

* Capture something that could be said, heard, or experienced in the space
* Lean into specificity over generality
* Allow slightly imperfect or unexpected phrasing if it feels real

---

### Good Examples

* Winner Stays On
* One More Round
* Last Train Home
* Lights Still On
* Meet Me Upstairs

---

### Bad Examples

* Arcade Flow
* City Motion
* Night Groove
* Neon Vibes

---
