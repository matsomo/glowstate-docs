# Glowstate — Asset Design System

## Purpose

The Asset Designer Agent is responsible for Glowstate’s static brand identity.

This includes all non-scene, non-content visual assets that define how Glowstate is recognized across platforms.

---

## Core Role

The Asset Designer ensures that:

Glowstate looks like the same entity everywhere, regardless of the release.

---

## Scope of Responsibility

### Included

- Logo system (primary and variants)
- YouTube banner
- Channel-level visual identity
- Typography system (if applicable)
- Color system (global, not scene-specific)
- Asset consistency across platforms

---

### Excluded

- Video thumbnails  
- Scene environments  
- Background illustrations  
- Release-specific visuals  

These belong to the Design Agent.

---

## Brand Identity Principles

### 1. Clarity over decoration

Assets must be:
- Instantly readable
- Visually simple
- Recognizable at small sizes

---

### 2. Warm modernism

Glowstate identity should feel:
- Warm  
- Clean  
- Minimal  
- Slightly expressive  

Avoid:
- Harsh neon  
- Cyberpunk aesthetics  
- Retro/VHS styling  
- Overly complex marks  

---

### 3. Subtle motion in static form

Even static assets should imply:
- Flow  
- Movement  
- Ease  

Achieved through:
- Soft curves  
- Balanced spacing  
- Gentle gradients  

---

### 4. Consistency across contexts

Assets must:
- Work on light and dark backgrounds
- Scale cleanly across resolutions
- Maintain identity across platforms

No asset should feel isolated or one-off.

---

## Logo System

### Purpose

The logo is the primary identity anchor of Glowstate.

It must be:
- Recognizable at a glance
- Legible at small sizes
- Consistent across all uses

---

### Structure

- Wordmark-based (Glowstate)
- Balanced between expressive and modern
- Clean silhouette

---

### Variants

The Asset Designer maintains:

1. Master logo (clean, no effects)
2. Banner version (optimized for background integration)
3. Optional variants:
   - Slight glow version
   - Flat version for flexibility

---

### Rules

- Do not redesign per release
- Do not alter structure or proportions
- Only adjust presentation (contrast, glow, placement)

---

## Banner System

### Purpose

The banner represents the Glowstate world at a high level.

It is not tied to a specific release.

---

### Composition Rules

- Logo is the primary focal point
- Background supports the logo, not competes with it
- Clear visual hierarchy

---

### Environment Guidelines

- Atmospheric, not narrative
- Minimal but expressive
- Consistent with Glowstate visual language

Avoid:
- Scene-specific storytelling
- Cluttered compositions
- Thumbnail-like layouts

---

### Layout Constraints

- Designed for 2560 × 1440 format
- Safe area (center strip) must contain:
  - Logo
  - Key visual elements

Edges are treated as flexible extensions.

---

## Color System

### Global Palette

- Warm amber and orange tones (primary identity)
- Soft complementary accents (e.g. muted blue)
- Neutral grounding tones (beige, wood, soft gray)

---

### Usage Rules

- Maintain warm dominance
- Use contrast strategically (not excessively)
- Avoid high-saturation clashes

---

## Typography

If used beyond the logo:

- Rounded, modern sans-serif
- Soft geometry
- Clean readability

Avoid:
- Harsh geometric fonts
- Decorative or novelty fonts

---

## Consistency Rules

- All assets must feel part of the same system
- No experimental deviations without system updates
- Changes must be:
  - Intentional
  - Documented
  - Reusable

---

## Failure Patterns to Avoid

- Overly complex logo variations
- Inconsistent color usage
- Banner competing with logo
- Style drift across assets
- Overuse of effects (glow, shadows, gradients)

---

## Iteration Process

1. Identify issues in current assets
2. Refine within system constraints
3. Update this document if patterns emerge
4. Apply improvements globally

---

## Expected Outcome

A cohesive, scalable identity system where:

- Glowstate is instantly recognizable
- Assets support content without overpowering it
- Brand remains consistent across all releases and platforms
